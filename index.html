Hello World!

This is my first website and here's a paragraph about me. Starting off with my early college career, I was stuck between wanting to go into pharmacy or UX/UI design but ultimately went into community college with a pharmacy degree in mind. At community college, I finished my core classes before transferring over to UTD to switch into UX/UI where I’m now working on completing my major classes. I ultimately came to the conclusion of going down the design route because I realized that I was always taking graphic design classes in middle and high school. Along with those classes, I was never academically focused in science, so I knew that 8 years of pharmacy school would not do well for me. Outside of school, I enjoy writing and reading fiction stories, drawing characters, and watching shows, movies, and Youtube videos during my freetime.

This is a list of my inspirational websites~

  • Pokemon Explorer

    This link provides statistical information for any Pokemon from generation one through nine. When looking at a Pokemon’s stats, the website also offers a list of Pokemon that are of similar type. I find inspiration from this website through how much quick information it’s able to give us. I tend to enjoy seeing statistical information laid out; especially when it comes to games or characters.

  • Minimal Wim

    Minimum Wim was a really cool website to me. The concept of designing a font based on someone’s work is amazing. According to the website, Wim Crouwel was one of the first people to work on Dutch graphic designs. He is best known for his grid-based typography. The creators of this website show us their process of how they designed a grid-based font through a step-by-step example of their design process. I found it really inspiring to see their 3x3 grid turn into letters, but as I kept scrolling, I eventually found it difficult to read their typeface.

  • r/assholedesign

    A classic website: Reddit. While I don’t use it everyday, there are still moments in my life where I’ll scavenge through reddit feeds for answers to my questions. This feed specifically is helpful for knowing what some designers have created that users don’t find appealing or helpful; making it a useful inspirational tool.
